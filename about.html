WHAT MAKES THE LION DIFFERENT THEY HUNT DURING STORMS

Lions do most of their hunting at night as their eyes have adapted to the dark and this gives them a huge advantage over their prey. Interestingly though, lions also hunt during storms. The noise, rain and/or wind make it harder for prey to see and hear them, increasing their chances of a successful hunt.

When the thunder's roaring and the rain's pouring down, that lion doesn't back down. It steps up and takes what it wants. Now, each and every one of us can channel that lion spirit. When life throws its storms our way, we don't run and hide. We rise up! We seize the moment! We show the world what we're made of!
